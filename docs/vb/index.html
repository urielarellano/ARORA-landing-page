Fitness Scientist Reveals Secrets to Keeping High Energy and Motivation for Physical Training

One of the biggest fitness struggles for people over 60 is having ENERGY for exercise.

That’s why Dr. Micowlow, who’s trained over 207 seniors and helped some lose up to 60lb of fat (others gained 15lb of muscle), is sharing the best strategies to keep your energy high in the gym.

1. Cut your workouts in half!

Studies have shown that just doing 1-2 sets per muscle group per workout is enough to get ~80% of the muscle growth of an optimized workout, while getting 100% of the health benefits.

If you’re struggling with energy, consider doing a bit less, and you’ll still get most of the benefits.

2. Avoid Added Sugar at Least 4 Days a Week!

Eating added sugar in the morning causes a glucose spike, which gives you a short spike of energy but then causes a crash around the middle of the day.

If you ever feel tired or lazy at around 1-4pm, this is likely why.

Try avoiding added sugar for 3-4 days a week, especially on workout days, and see how it affects your energy.
